# Code of Conduct

## Our commitment

We want MD+HTML Reader to be a respectful, constructive place for users and contributors of every background and experience level. We commit to making participation free from harassment, discrimination, personal attacks, and intimidation.

## Expected behavior

- Be respectful, specific, and open to good-faith disagreement.
- Focus review and discussion on ideas, evidence, and the project.
- Welcome newcomers and help them understand project context.
- Respect privacy: never post confidential documents, credentials, or personal information without permission.
- Accept responsibility for mistakes and help repair their impact.

## Unacceptable behavior

- Harassment, hate speech, threats, stalking, or unwanted sexual attention.
- Insults, trolling, dogpiling, or attacks on a person rather than their contribution.
- Publishing private information, credentials, or confidential document content without permission.
- Deliberately disrupting discussions, reviews, or project spaces.

## Scope and enforcement

This Code of Conduct applies in repository issues, pull requests, reviews, discussions, and other project spaces. Maintainers may remove content, close or lock conversations, reject contributions, or restrict participation when behavior violates these standards.

## Reporting

Do not put sensitive details in a public issue. For public content that violates this Code, use GitHub's report option on the relevant item. If a private maintainer reporting channel is published on the repository profile, use that channel instead. Reports should include the relevant link, a concise description, and any context needed to assess the situation.

This document is adapted from the principles of the Contributor Covenant, version 2.1.
